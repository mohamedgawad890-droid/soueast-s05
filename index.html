DUAL 10.25″ SCREEN PANEL

What You're Looking At

The S05 features two 10.25-inch screens mounted side-by-side to create one continuous display panel — a digital instrument cluster on the left and a touch infotainment screen on the right. Together they give the dashboard a modern, high-tech appearance.

Left Screen – Digital Cluster

  • Shows speed, RPM, fuel level, engine temp
  • Navigation arrows can appear here when using maps on the right screen
  • Driver assistance indicators and warnings display here
  • Some display themes available — check Settings → Display

Right Screen – Infotainment Touch

  • Main interface for music, navigation, phone, vehicle settings
  • Supports Apple CarPlay, Android Auto, and native apps
  • Touch-operated climate zone — tap the A/C icon at the bottom
  • Swipe between home screens; pinch to zoom on maps
⚠️ Tip: The touch-based climate controls can be distracting while driving. Consider adjusting temperature before you start a trip or when safely stopped.
APPLE CARPLAY & ANDROID AUTO
✅ The S05 supports both WIRELESS and wired CarPlay/Android Auto. No cable needed — just pair once via Bluetooth and your phone connects automatically every time.

How to Connect Wirelessly – iPhone (CarPlay)

  • On the infotainment screen go to: Settings → CarPlay → Add device
  • On your iPhone go to Settings → General → CarPlay and select the car
  • After the first pairing, CarPlay connects automatically every time you start the car
  • Alternatively, connect via USB-C or Lightning cable for wired CarPlay

How to Connect – Android (Android Auto)

  • Install the Android Auto app from the Play Store (if not already installed)
  • For wireless: enable Wi-Fi and Bluetooth on your phone, then select the car from Android Auto settings
  • For wired: connect your phone via USB cable. Allow all requested permissions on the phone

What You Can Do With It

  • Navigation: Google Maps, Waze, Apple Maps — on the big screen
  • Music: Spotify, YouTube Music, Apple Music, and more
  • Phone calls: hands-free calling using your contacts
  • Messages: Siri or Google Assistant reads and replies to messages for you

USB Ports & Wireless Charging

  • 5 USB ports total — front console + rear passengers
  • Wireless phone charging pad in the center console — place your phone and it charges automatically
  • The wireless charger has a built-in ventilation fan to prevent overheating while charging
✅ Best practice: Use wireless CarPlay + wireless charging for a fully cable-free experience. Keep the charging pad free of metal objects.
CLIMATE CONTROL GUIDE

Automatic Climate Control

The S05 has a fully automatic single-zone climate control system. Set your desired temperature and the car will automatically adjust the fan speed and airflow direction to reach and maintain it.

Controls on the Touchscreen

  • Temperature +/– : Use the arrows at the bottom of the climate panel
  • AUTO mode: Tap AUTO to let the system manage everything
  • Fan speed: Manual override — tap the fan icon and use +/–
  • A/C ON/OFF: Toggle the snowflake icon
  • Recirculation: Tap the circular arrow icon — useful in traffic or dusty roads
  • Rear defogger: Tap the rear window icon to clear fogged rear glass
💡 In summer, set 22–24°C on AUTO for efficient cooling. In winter, 24–26°C. The AUTO mode is more fuel-efficient than manually running at full fan.

6-Speaker Sound System

The S05 comes with a 6-speaker audio system. It delivers clear and balanced sound for everyday listening.

CAMERA SYSTEM GUIDE

360° Around View Monitor

The S05 uses four cameras to create a bird's-eye view of the car and its surroundings. This appears automatically when you engage reverse gear.

  • Tap any of the four camera views to switch to that angle
  • Parking guide lines show the predicted car path based on steering angle
  • You can also manually activate the camera via the screen while driving forward

Rear Camera (Reverse)

When you shift into Reverse, the rear camera activates automatically with dynamic guide lines. Green lines = safe zone. Yellow = caution. Red = stop.

💡 Keep your cameras clean. Dirty lenses produce blurry, misleading images. Clean with a soft cloth and water.

DEFROST & FOG GUIDE
ℹ️ In rain and cold weather, fog builds up on the windscreen and windows. The S05 has two dedicated buttons in the climate panel to deal with this quickly.

Button 1 (Orange) — Rear Window & Mirror Heater

  • Located in the climate panel next to the temperature control buttons
  • Activates real electric heating elements embedded in the rear glass — evaporates condensation and water
  • Also heats the side mirrors — clears water and fog from the areas you look through to see behind you
  • Works independently of the A/C — effective even with engine just started

Button 2 (Green) — Front Windscreen Defroster

  • Redirects the A/C air vents (top of dashboard) toward the front windscreen
  • Also directs air to the side windows — the narrow areas beside the side mirrors
  • For best results: set A/C temperature to medium-warm (halfway between middle and hot) before pressing
  • Warm air is far more effective at clearing fog than cold air
💡 Rainy day routine: Press Button 1 (rear heater + mirrors) first. Then press Button 2 and set temperature to medium-warm. Visibility clears in under 2 minutes.
ACTIVE & PASSIVE SAFETY

Safety Systems

Understanding all safety features on your Soueast S05

AIRBAGS – PASSIVE SAFETY

4-Airbag System

  • 2 Front Airbags – deploy in frontal collisions to protect driver and front passenger
  • 2 Side Airbags – protect the torso in side-impact crashes for driver and front passenger
🚨 Never sit a child in the front seat if the passenger airbag is active. Seat children in the rear with appropriate child seats.
ACTIVE SAFETY SYSTEMS
🔒
ABS + EBD
Anti-lock Braking prevents wheel lock-up during hard braking. EBD distributes braking force between front and rear axles for balanced stopping.
⚖️
ESC – Electronic Stability Control
Detects and reduces loss of traction. ESC applies individual brakes and reduces engine power to keep you on course.
🕐
Hill Start Assist
Holds the brakes for up to 2 seconds on a slope, giving you time to move your foot to the accelerator without rolling backwards.
🅿️
Auto Hold
Keeps the car stationary without holding the brake pedal — useful in traffic. The car releases automatically when you press the accelerator.
👁️
Blind Spot Warning
Radar sensors monitor lanes next to you. A warning icon illuminates on the corresponding side mirror when a vehicle is in your blind spot.
↩️
Rear Cross Traffic Alert
When reversing, detects vehicles approaching from either side and warns you before a collision.
🏎️
Traction Control (TCS)
Prevents wheel spin when accelerating on slippery surfaces. Works automatically.
⬇️
Electric Parking Brake (EPB)
Replaces the traditional handbrake. Press the P button on center console when parked. Integrated with Auto Hold.
ℹ️ Most active safety systems work automatically. If any system malfunctions, a warning light will appear on your dashboard.

TYRE PRESSURE GUIDE

Recommended Pressure — 215/60 R17

30–31

  • Always check tyre pressure when tyres are COLD (before driving or after 3+ hours parked)
  • Pressure naturally rises when tyres heat up — do not deflate hot tyres
  • Under-inflated tyres increase fuel consumption by up to 3% and cause faster edge wear
  • Over-inflated tyres cause faster centre wear and reduce grip
HOW TO RESET THE SERVICE REMINDER

When the service warning light appears — steps to reset it yourself

  • Step 1: Car OFF and screens dark → press Start ONCE without pressing the brake pedal
  • Step 2: Hold Start + OK button on steering wheel together for ~15 seconds until indicators appear on the cluster
  • Step 3: Start the car normally — the service and maintenance warning lights will be gone
⚠️ Only reset the service reminder AFTER the actual service has been done.
WEEKLY CHECKS YOU SHOULD DO
  • Check tyre pressure when cold — recommended 30–31 PSI (2.1 bar) for 215/60 R17
  • Check engine oil level via the dipstick — oil should be between MIN and MAX
  • Check windshield washer fluid level — especially important in dusty conditions
  • Inspect all lights for any blown bulbs
  • Visually inspect tyres for any cuts, bulges, or foreign objects
💡 Egypt's heat and dust are hard on cars. Change your cabin air filter every 10,000 km, and always check coolant level in summer.
GET MORE FROM EVERY DRIVE

Driving Tips

Fuel saving, smooth driving, and getting the best out of the 1.5T DCT-6

FUEL SAVING TIPS
1
Smooth Acceleration
The 6-speed DCT is designed for smooth, progressive acceleration. Avoid flooring the pedal from standstill. Accelerate progressively.
2
Use ECO Mode for City Driving
Switch to ECO mode in city traffic — it softens throttle response and optimises gear shifts for lower fuel use. Ideal for Cairo traffic.
3
Maintain Steady Highway Speed
Use Cruise Control at 100–110 km/h for maximum efficiency. The S05 is most fuel-efficient between 80–110 km/h.
4
Coast to Slow Down
Lift off the accelerator early and let the car coast. DCT engine braking slows you down with near-zero fuel use.
5
Tyre Pressure Matters
Under-inflated tyres increase fuel consumption by up to 3%. Maintain 30–31 PSI (2.1 bar) for the 215/60 R17 tyres.
6
A/C Smart Usage
At low speeds, consider windows down. At highway speeds above 60 km/h, A/C is more efficient than open windows.
DRIVE MODES

Normal / Sport / ECO

  • Normal — balanced mode for everyday driving. Good blend of performance and fuel economy.
  • Sport — sharpens throttle response and holds gears longer. Fuel consumption increases.
  • ECO — softens throttle and shifts up earlier for lowest fuel use. Best for city traffic and long commutes.
DCT TRANSMISSION TIPS

Understanding Your 6-Speed DCT

The DCT uses two separate clutches for odd/even gears — lightning-fast shifts with no power interruption. Faster and more efficient than a regular automatic.

  • DCT may feel slightly jerky at very low speeds (parking lot manoeuvres) — this is normal
  • Never hold the car on an incline using the accelerator — use Auto Hold instead
  • DCT fluid should be changed at 50,000 km as per the official service schedule
  • In Sport mode, DCT holds lower gears longer — great for overtaking but uses more fuel
REMOTE START ACTIVATION

If Remote Start Isn't Working — Re-program Your Key Fob

Sometimes the key fob receives the pairing code but hasn't been fully programmed to the car. The fix is simple:

  • Press the LOCK button 3–4 times in quick succession (normal presses)
  • Press the UNLOCK button 3–4 times in quick succession (normal presses)
  • Press LOCK once (normal press) → then immediately press and hold the REMOTE START button (long press)
  • Repeat the last step multiple times until the remote start activates
💡 Use remote start in summer to pre-cool the cabin before you get in. The car will run for a set time then shut off automatically.
CITY & TRAFFIC TIPS
  • Use Auto Hold in stop-and-go traffic — no need to hold the brake pedal
  • Use the 360° camera when parking in tight streets
  • Switch to recirculation mode on dusty roads to stop dust entering the cabin
  • Watch for the Blind Spot Warning light when changing lanes
  • Hill Start Assist activates automatically on inclines
