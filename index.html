DUAL 10.25″ SCREEN PANEL

What You're Looking At

The S05 features two 10.25-inch screens mounted side-by-side to create one continuous display panel — a digital instrument cluster on the left and a touch infotainment screen on the right. Together they give the dashboard a modern, high-tech appearance.

Left Screen – Digital Cluster

  • Shows speed, RPM, fuel level, engine temp
  • Navigation arrows can appear here when using maps on the right screen
  • Driver assistance indicators and warnings display here
  • Some display themes available — check Settings → Display

Right Screen – Infotainment Touch

  • Main interface for music, navigation, phone, vehicle settings
  • Supports Apple CarPlay, Android Auto, and native apps
  • Touch-operated climate zone — tap the A/C icon at the bottom
  • Swipe between home screens; pinch to zoom on maps
⚠️ Tip: The touch-based climate controls can be distracting while driving. Consider adjusting temperature before you start a trip or when safely stopped.
APPLE CARPLAY & ANDROID AUTO

How to Connect – iPhone (CarPlay)

  • Connect your iPhone with a USB-C or Lightning cable to the USB port in the center console
  • A CarPlay permission prompt will appear on your iPhone — tap Allow
  • CarPlay will launch automatically on the infotainment screen
  • Press the Home button on screen to return to the CarPlay menu

How to Connect – Android (Android Auto)

  • Install the Android Auto app from the Play Store (if not already installed)
  • Connect your phone via USB cable. Allow all requested permissions on the phone
  • Android Auto will launch on the infotainment screen automatically

What You Can Do With It

  • Navigation: Google Maps, Waze, Apple Maps — on the big screen
  • Music: Spotify, YouTube Music, Apple Music, and more
  • Phone calls: hands-free calling using your contacts
  • Messages: Siri or Google Assistant reads and replies to messages for you
✅ Best practice: Keep your phone plugged in so it charges while you navigate — GPS and screen mirroring drain the battery quickly.
CLIMATE CONTROL GUIDE

Automatic Climate Control

The S05 has a fully automatic single-zone climate control system. Set your desired temperature and the car will automatically adjust the fan speed and airflow direction to reach and maintain it.

Controls on the Touchscreen

  • Temperature +/– : Use the arrows at the bottom of the climate panel
  • AUTO mode: Tap AUTO to let the system manage everything
  • Fan speed: Manual override — tap the fan icon and use +/–
  • A/C ON/OFF: Toggle the snowflake icon
  • Recirculation: Tap the circular arrow icon — useful in traffic or dusty roads
  • Rear defogger: Tap the rear window icon to clear fogged rear glass
💡 In summer, set 22–24°C on AUTO for efficient cooling. In winter, 24–26°C. The AUTO mode is more fuel-efficient than manually running at full fan.
AUDIO & BLUETOOTH

6-Speaker Sound System

The S05 comes with a 6-speaker audio system. It delivers clear and balanced sound for everyday listening.

Pairing Your Phone via Bluetooth

  • On the infotainment screen: Phone → Bluetooth → Add device
  • On your phone, enable Bluetooth and select 'Soueast S05' from the device list
  • Confirm the pairing code shown on both screens
  • Once paired, your phone will automatically connect when you start the car

Audio Sources Available

📻 AM/FM Radio 📱 Bluetooth Audio 🔌 USB Music 🍎 CarPlay 🤖 Android Auto
CAMERA SYSTEM GUIDE

360° Around View Monitor

The S05 uses four cameras to create a bird's-eye view of the car and its surroundings. This appears automatically when you engage reverse gear.

  • Tap any of the four camera views to switch to that angle
  • Parking guide lines show the predicted car path based on steering angle
  • You can also manually activate the camera via the screen while driving forward

Rear Camera (Reverse)

When you shift into Reverse, the rear camera activates automatically with dynamic guide lines. Green lines = safe zone. Yellow = caution. Red = stop.

💡 Keep your cameras clean. Dirty lenses produce blurry, misleading images. Clean with a soft cloth and water.
ACTIVE & PASSIVE SAFETY

Safety Systems

Understanding all safety features on your Soueast S05

AIRBAGS – PASSIVE SAFETY

6-Airbag System

  • 2 Front Airbags – deploy in frontal collisions to protect driver and front passenger
  • 2 Side Airbags – protect the torso in side-impact crashes
  • 2 Curtain Airbags – deploy from the roofline to protect heads on both rows
🚨 Never sit a child in the front seat if the passenger airbag is active. Seat children in the rear with ISOFIX child seats.
ACTIVE SAFETY SYSTEMS
🔒
ABS + EBD
Anti-lock Braking prevents wheel lock-up during hard braking. EBD distributes braking force between front and rear axles for balanced stopping.
⚖️
ESC – Electronic Stability Control
Detects and reduces loss of traction. ESC applies individual brakes and reduces engine power to keep you on course.
🕐
Hill Start Assist
Holds the brakes for up to 2 seconds on a slope, giving you time to move your foot to the accelerator without rolling backwards.
🅿️
Auto Hold
Keeps the car stationary without holding the brake pedal — useful in traffic. The car releases automatically when you press the accelerator.
👁️
Blind Spot Warning
Radar sensors monitor lanes next to you. A warning icon illuminates on the corresponding side mirror when a vehicle is in your blind spot.
↩️
Rear Cross Traffic Alert
When reversing, detects vehicles approaching from either side and warns you before a collision.
🏎️
Traction Control (TCS)
Prevents wheel spin when accelerating on slippery surfaces. Works automatically.
⬇️
Electric Parking Brake (EPB)
Replaces the traditional handbrake. Press the P button on center console when parked. Integrated with Auto Hold.
ℹ️ Most active safety systems work automatically. If any system malfunctions, a warning light will appear on your dashboard.

KEEP IT RUNNING WELL

Maintenance Guide

Service intervals and care tips for your Soueast S05

SERVICE SCHEDULE
🛢️
Engine Oil & Filter Change
Use 5W-30 or 5W-40 fully synthetic oil. Change the oil filter at the same time.
Every 7,500 km
🌬️
Air Filter (Engine)
Inspect at 20,000 km, replace if dirty. More frequent in dusty environments.
Every 20,000–30,000 km
🧹
Cabin Air Filter (A/C Filter)
Filters dust and pollen entering the cabin. Essential to replace in dusty cities.
Every 10,000–15,000 km
⚡
Spark Plugs
Iridium or platinum plugs. A good set lasts much longer than standard copper plugs.
Every 60,000 km
🔴
Brake Fluid
DOT 4 brake fluid. Must be flushed regularly to maintain braking performance.
Every 2 years or 40,000 km
🌡️
Coolant / Antifreeze
Keep at the correct level. Top up with distilled water + coolant mix only.
Check monthly / Replace at 60,000 km
🔋
Car Battery
Standard 12V lead-acid battery. Heat shortens battery life. Have it tested annually after year 2.
Replace every 3–4 years
🔩
Tyre Rotation & Inspection
Rotate front and rear tyres to extend life. Minimum tread depth: 1.6mm.
Every 10,000–15,000 km
WEEKLY CHECKS YOU SHOULD DO
  • Check tyre pressure when tyres are cold. See the sticker inside your driver door.
  • Check engine oil level via the dipstick — oil should be between MIN and MAX
  • Check windshield washer fluid level — especially important in dusty environments
  • Inspect all lights for any blown bulbs
  • Visually inspect tyres for any cuts, bulges, or foreign objects
💡 Egypt's heat and dust are hard on cars. Change your cabin air filter every 10,000 km maximum, and always check coolant level in summer.

Driving Tips

Fuel saving, smooth driving, and getting the best out of the 1.5T CVT

FUEL SAVING TIPS
1
Smooth Acceleration
The CVT is designed for smooth acceleration. Avoid flooring the pedal from standstill. Accelerate progressively.
2
Maintain Steady Highway Speed
Use Cruise Control at 100–110 km/h for maximum efficiency. The S05 is most fuel-efficient between 80–110 km/h.
3
Coast to Slow Down
Lift off the accelerator early and let the car coast. CVT engine braking slows you down with near-zero fuel use.
4
Tyre Pressure Matters
Under-inflated tyres increase fuel consumption by up to 3%. Maintain recommended pressure.
5
A/C Smart Usage
At low speeds, consider windows down. At highway speeds above 60 km/h, A/C is more efficient than open windows.
CVT TRANSMISSION TIPS

Understanding Your CVT

The CVT has no fixed gears — it continuously adjusts the ratio for optimal efficiency. This is different from a regular automatic.

  • Never hold the engine at high revs for long — it strains the CVT belt
  • The 'rubber band' effect (engine revving before acceleration) is normal for CVT
  • When carrying heavy loads, the CVT works harder — give it time to cool down after
  • CVT fluid should be checked by a technician every 60,000–80,000 km
CITY & TRAFFIC TIPS
  • Use Auto Hold in stop-and-go traffic — no need to hold the brake pedal
  • Use the 360° camera when parking in tight streets
  • Switch to recirculation mode on dusty roads to stop dust entering the cabin
  • Watch for the Blind Spot Warning light when changing lanes
  • Hill Start Assist activates automatically on inclines
